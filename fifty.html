This video leads through the design of the desktop and mobile version of the website



The website is made for a theater group named Fifty-ish where all
the members are in their 50s.
The target group for the show is the same as for the website:
It's interesting for young (from their 20s) and adult.

I was also responsible for the photography,
all the group pictures on homepage are made and edited by me.

Tools

Adobe Photoshop
Adobe XD

Goal:

The goal is to make unique user interface design
that is modern and shows the spirit of the show.


Work process

First, there was a research made and styleboard
created. Main features were: Fun, Clean and Sleek.
Graphic designer Isabel Rinkel, with whom I worked together
on the styleboard, was responsible for the logo design and posters.
Together we created a styleboard, where we used a dark theme
with bright color accents.
We came up with an idea of using warm color like orange, that is
associated with warmth and fun.
Our goal was to create an elegant and clean style that also captures
the identity of the group.
With the help of photography I captured the spirit of the show.

Then, I made some idea-sketches and couple of concepts.
I made a couple of concepts of every page
and showed this to client.
As following, I worked out the wireframes and concept
and sent it to back-end developer, who also worked for Fifty-ish.
